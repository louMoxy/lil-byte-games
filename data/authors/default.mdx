Hi, I'm Louise.

I'm a frontend developer based in London — over ten years in the industry, which is simultaneously impressive and slightly alarming. By night I'm attempting to become an indie game developer, which is going about as smoothly as you'd expect.

I grew up loving games — quietly, on my own, because in the 90s girls weren't really supposed to. I loved them anyway. The problem solving, the worlds, the feeling of making progress at something. That never left me, even when life went in very different directions. _(I did a choreography degree once. But does mean I am great on the dancefloor.)_

I've been learning Unity for about four years now. I've done too many courses, survived some game jams, and spent more evenings than I'd like to admit trying to code after a full day of already coding. I'm now building my first real game — a space shooter — and documenting the whole thing here. The good bits, the bad bits, and the bits where I stare at the screen and genuinely don't know what I'm doing.

## Life Stuff

When I'm not at a screen I'm usually dancing, catching a gig or comedy night somewhere in London, or hunting down a good vegan meal and a great drink. I'm a sucker for old films — I even go to a film trash club, which is exactly what it sounds like and I highly recommend it.

I have two dogs. They are old. They are cute. They require a lot of walks, which I love, and a lot of vet visits, which I do not.

I keep my circle small but I look after the people in it. My friends would probably describe me as fun, caring, and someone who is always learning something new — which tracks, given the amount of money I've spent on Udemy courses.

## The Work Bit

By day I specialise in React, TypeScript, and complex UI problems. I started out self-studying while working multiple minimum wage jobs, built free websites for charities to get portfolio pieces, and worked my way up from there. I know how to figure things out the hard way.

I like making things. Always have. That's really what all of this comes down to.
